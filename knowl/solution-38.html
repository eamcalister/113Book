Solution 5.2.20.1.

The first thing we should do is draw a well labeled picture of the situation. Since she has \(100\) yards of fence to use, we have the relation
\begin{equation*} x+2w = 100. \end{equation*}
The area is given by \(A = xw\text{.}\) To obtain the area as a function of \(x\text{,}\) we substitute \(w=\frac{100-x}{2}\) (from the constraint) for \(w\) to get
\begin{equation*} A(x) = x\left(\frac{100-x}{2}\right) = -\frac{x^2}{2}+50x. \end{equation*}
When we find the vertex form of this function we find \(h=50\) and \(-\frac{h^2}{2} + k = 0\text{,}\) hence \(k = 1250\text{.}\) Thus
\begin{equation*} A(x) = -\frac{1}{2}(x-50)^2 + 1250. \end{equation*}
This means the maximum area is \(1,250\) yd\({^2}\text{,}\) which occurs when \(x=50\) and \(w = 25\text{.}\)
