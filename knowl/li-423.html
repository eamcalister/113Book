Is \(Q\) multiplied by an additional factor of \(1.25\) each time \(t\) increases by \(20\text{.}\) Yes, observe
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} f(2) & = & 3\\ f(22) & = & 3(1.25)^{\frac{20}{20}} = 3(1.25)\\ f(42) & = & 3(1.25)^{\frac{40}{20}} = 3(1.25)^2\\ \ & \vdots & \\\end{aligned} \end{equation*}
