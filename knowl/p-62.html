Since taking \(b\) steps of size \(\frac{a}{b}\) reaches \(a\text{,}\) we have the following identity:
\begin{equation*} b\cdot\frac{a}{b} = a. \end{equation*}
When \(a\) and \(b\) are not integers, we use this identity to what \(\frac{a}{b}\) is: For any numbers \(a\) and \(b\text{,}\) \(\frac{a}{b}\) is the unique number such that when it is multiplied by \(b\text{,}\) the result is \(a\text{.}\) In other words,multiplication by \(b\) is the inverse operation of dividing by \(b\text{.}\) Likewise, division is the inverse operation of multiplication.
