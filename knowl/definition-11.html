Definition 5.3.14. Percent Growth Rate Form of an Exponential Function.

Every exponential function \(f(x) = ab^{x}\) may be written in the form
\begin{equation*} f(x) = a(1+r)^{x}. \end{equation*}
In this form \(r = b-1\) is the percent growth (when multiplied by \(100\%\)) for each unit increase in \(x\text{.}\) Note:
  • If \(b>1\text{,}\) then \(r>0\text{.}\) In this case \(r\) is just called the growth rate.
  • If \(0<b<1\text{,}\) then \(r<0\text{.}\) In this case \(|r|\) is the decay rate.
