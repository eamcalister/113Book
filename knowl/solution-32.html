Solution 5.1.9.1.

Observe that the units of \(\$ /\text{widget}\) on the selling price and production cost are both rates of change of the form \((\text{function output})/(\text{function input})\text{.}\) This indicates the function is likely a linear function. Going to the formula we get
\begin{equation*} P = (\text{revenue}) - (\text{cost}) = 35q - (1000 + 15q). \end{equation*}
This simplifies to the linear function
\begin{equation*} P = 20q - 1000. \end{equation*}
Here we see that \(\$ 1000\) will be spent no matter how many widgets are sold, and we get a net profit of \(\$ 20\) for each widget sold.
