In this figure we see that the total shaded area is \(x^2+y^2\) while the area of the entire square is \((x+y)^2\text{.}\) If you note that the unshaded portions of the square each have area \(xy\text{,}\) we can see that \((x+y)^2\) is equivalent to \(x^2+2xy+y^2\) (That can be seen symbollically using the distributive law 2.2.19, which we will see soon.).
