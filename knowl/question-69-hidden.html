When \(f(x) = ax^2 + bx + c = a(x-r)(x-s)\text{,}\) we can use the factors \((x-r)\) and \((x-s)\) to really see what the graph of \(f\) looks like, and how \(a\) influences it. In what follows, suppose \(r\) and \(s\) are generic real numbers with \(r<s\text{.}\)
  1. On a single set of axes graph the lines \(y = x-r\) and \(y = x-s\text{.}\) Use this graph to find the values of \(x\) where \(x-r\) and \(x-s\) have the same sign and where they have different signs. Use this do decide when \(y=(x-r)(x-s)\) is positive and when it is negative.
  2. Use your answers from part (a) to sketch a graph of \(y = (x-r)(x-s)\text{.}\)
  3. Using your graph from part (b) and reasoning from part (a), explain why the sign of the parameter \(a\) tells you whether the graph of \(f(x) = a(x-r)(x-s)\) opens up or down.
